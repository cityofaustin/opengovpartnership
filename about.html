About the Open Government Partnership

An international coalition of government and civil society, the Open Government Partnership (OGP) began in 2011 with 8 countries and grew to 70 countries by 2016.

Being a member means co-creating concrete commitments with civil society. OGP helps with support and accountability.

OGP Values

Visit our Bloomfire site to read more in-depth about OGP's values.

  • Tech & Innovation

    Embrace the role of new technologies, open access to technologies, & increase citizens’ capacity to use technology

  • Transparency

    Publish information on government activities and decisions in a comprehensive, timely, and freely and open manner

  • Accountability

    Justify government actions, act upon criticisms or requirements made, and accept responsibility for failure to perform

  • Civic Participation

    Mobilize citizens in public debate, provide input, and make contributions responsive, innovative and effective governance

OGP in Austin

In 2015, OGP opened membership to “subnational” or governments below the national level. The pilot will help OGP learn about supporting local governments worldwide.

    “Open government initiatives at the subnational level potentially generate more impact, have greater visibility and are easier to measure.”

Austin applied through a rigorous application process requiring: evidence of City Council support, letters of recommendation, community endorsements, commitments to collaborate from civil society organizations

Austin is the only U.S. subnational government to join the pilot.
